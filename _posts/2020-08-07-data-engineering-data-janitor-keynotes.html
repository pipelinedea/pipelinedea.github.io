I don't like to talk. I don't like to talk at conferences. I'm an introvert at heart. Still, sometimes one has to step out of the comfort zone and stand for things that deserve to be stood for. All my talks were born this way, some more technical, some more reflective on the practicalities of data life.

In 2016, at Berlin Buzzwords in Berlin I shared our journey of how we migrated a data stack from AWS to Azure. How we managed to do it with open sourcing tools as roadkills and what we’ve learnt about the barebone necessities ending up within the sole body of a Raspberry Pi may shock the absolute believers of distributed computing. An adventure in bash, make and SQL with a detour in Moore's law and falling memory prices. You can find the slides right here.

Later that year I was honoured to give a keynote at the Data Natives conference. Solving data related problems with no or limited resources, be them time, money or skills is possible with decades old technology, free operating systems and cheap hardware - if it makes sense to spend a hundred bucks instead of tearing your hair out, I’ll say so. Click here for the slides.

Two years later in 2018, after delivering the previous talk in different places and having conversations with the audience, fellow sufferers, I updated the talk - also although some time has passed, but lots of things didn't change. "The Data Janitor Returns" keeps talking to the underdog, is pretty opinionated, but covers GDPR, deep learning and all the hype. To see the slides, click here.

I'm preparing on a new one on what makes or brakes a data engineer — join us to shape it!
