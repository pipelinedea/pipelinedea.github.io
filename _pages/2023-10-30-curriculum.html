Curriculum

Are you looking for a structured and battle-tested approach for learning data engineering?

Would you like to have a seasoned expert guide you through the course and answer all your questions?

Do you want to grow your skills together with a small cohort, and have AMAs with world-class guest speakers every week?

The course covers the most relevant and essential data and software engineering topics that enable you to start your journey as a professional data engineer or analytics engineer. The curriculum and its delivery are defined by our core values: sustainability, transparency, pragmatism and collaboration.

You bring curiosity, motivation, some experience with Python and SQL, and willingness to get your hands dirty. We bring the rest.

The main difference between the experiences is the amount of time that you allocate to your assignments and learning, but every participant has access to the same course materials. We recommend the full-time course for anyone who’s schedule allows participating in it, however we understand that full-time employees can’t dedicate more than four hours on a daily basis. Full-time and part-time participants learn together in the same cohort.

Data Engineering Course curriculum

The first two weeks of the full-time course are about mastering the expected preliminary knowledge required for the rest of the course. Prepwork happens based on a curated guided tour of nonproprietary online courses that focus on laying the groundwork for the most fundamental tools. The final days of the prepwork module are about implementing a homework project in order to put the newly acquired skills directly to use.
Part-time course participants can work on the prepwork assignments flexibly on their on schedule, and have to complete the homework before they join the course sessions (week #3).

Week #1:

Prepwork #1

Python 3 basics (4 days), SQL basics (1 day)

Week #2:

Prepwork #2

Command line interfaces (2 days), GitHub and filling the gaps (1 day), Homework (2 days)

Together with your fellow students you are going to solve problems and write code on a daily basis in a setting that resembles a real-life software engineering environment. In parallel to the main theme of each week, the schedule includes slots for coding assignments, sustainability case studies in data, career coaching and communication training, and sessions with expert guest speakers from data startups and large corporates to broaden your horizon when it comes to the data engineering landscape that surrounds you. The final week is about building your own data product to prove your state-of-the-art competencies.
As a part-time student, you’ll join the live sessions starting week #3 and you’ll dedicate four hours a day to learn all the key concepts, solve the most essential coding assignments, and get access to the expert keynotes and AMAs. You’ll have access to all the course materials that full-time students have, and building a capstone project is optional.

Week #3:

Data Acquisition

What makes a Data Engineer? Data acquisition, evaluation framework, maturity model first iteration. Logs, databases, 3rd party APIs, scraping. Entities. Research acquisition. Makefiles. Career coaching. Portfolio project sketch.

Week #4:

Telemetry

Telemetry. Google Analytics, 3rd party, adblocking, OS tools (Snowplow et al). Queues: SQS, Kinesis, PubSub, Kafka, lambda.

Week #5:

ELT/ETL

ETL or ELT? Architectures: Barebone, DBT, AWS, GCP, Cloud Agnostic. Getting interviews. Wunderlist case study. Prefect. dbt.

Week #6:

Datastores and Storage

DWH SQL. Facts, dimensions, normalization, pivoting, cubes, indexing, partitioning, distribution styles. Schemas: star, snowflake, DataVault. Storage. HDFS. Presentation briefing. NoSQL. Mark's benchmarks.

Week #7:

Data Warehousing and Computation

Storage, compute, Hadoop, Spark, Dask. Redshift walkthrough. Research. SQL 101. Saturn Cloud. Portfolio examples, portfolio sketch. Practice KPIs. Classic datawarehousing.

Week #8:

BI Tooling and Data Quality

Research of BI tooling. Data quality, PII, GDPR, CCPA. Great Expectations. Data modelling workshop. Data lineage, metadata, testing, monitoring.

Week #9:

Clouds and Data Stacks

Clouds: AWS, GCP, Azure. Essential component mapping and comparison. Niche computing: Fly, Datasette, PythonAnywhere. Stacks: classic, hot-cold, data lake. Sustainability. Window functions. Access control. Maturity model second iteration.Stack creation. Real-life case studies: Oberlo, Microsoft To-Do, Wunderlist.

Week #10:

DevOps and DataOps

Deploy. Infrastructure as code. Automation: CD, CI. Github Actions. Logging, monitoring. Data engineer job search list. Workflows and processes. Docker, Kubernetes.

Week #11:

Serving Data Online

Serving, serving ML. Datasette. Docker dissect, Flask API and assignment. Streamlit. Takehome test, learning paths.

Week #12:

Final Project

Implementing your final and comprehensive data product.

What we don’t teach: complex mathematical and statistical modeling, machine learning and deep learning concepts, NLP and data visualisation are not part of the training - however we are happy to share recommendations for further learning.
